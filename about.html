📖 Our Genesis: From Wild Meadow to Living Classroom

Rooted in Alchemical Tradition

YardAlchemy Botanicals was born on a misty spring morning in the Blue Ridge Mountains. What started as one family’s quest to heal compacted clay soil transformed into a sanctuary for organic pioneers, herbalists, and permaculture artists. Our founder, Willow Greenfield, a former soil microbiologist and biodynamic farmer, envisioned a space where gardening transcends hobby — a living alchemy that reconnects people to the cycles of decay and rebirth.

Over a decade later, we’ve grown into a 12-acre regenerative hub: no-till vegetable terraces, a medicinal herb spiral, food forest arboretum, and the region’s largest public worm composting facility. But more than infrastructure, we've built a philosophy: every gardener is an alchemist, turning waste into gold, sunlight into nourishment, and knowledge into resilience.

🌎 Mission + Regenerative Vision

Soil. Sovereignty. Sanctuary.

Our mission is to empower 1 million gardeners by 2030 with toxin-free techniques, seed independence, and climate-smart design. We believe each yard—from balcony pots to multi-acre homesteads—can become a carbon sink, pollinator refuge, and pharmacy. Through immersive workshops, digital almanacs, and land-based mentorship, YardAlchemy bridges ancestral practices with ecological science.

We operate a closed-loop nursery: all pots are biodegradable or recycled, water is harvested from rainfall, and plant waste becomes compost for the next generation. Our vision: a world where synthetic chemicals are obsolete, and every community has access to fresh food grown in living soil.

We are also a certified B Corp (pending 2025) and donate 5% of all proceeds to urban gardening initiatives in food deserts. Regeneration is not just an idea — it’s our daily practice.

📜 The Deeper Alchemy: Our Origin Codex

✦ Forging a Sanctuary: 2012 – 2025 ✦

Before YardAlchemy Botanicals became a destination, the land was an abandoned pasture, compacted by heavy machinery, its topsoil nearly lifeless. Determined to prove that any landscape could be restored, Willow Greenfield and a handful of volunteers began a decade-long restoration: sheet mulching with cardboard, layering compost, planting dynamic accumulators like comfrey and borage. By 2016, the first fruit trees thrived without irrigation. By 2020, we had established the first public "Alchemy Folk School" teaching workshops from mushroom log inoculation to biodynamic calendar preparation.

Our approach is rooted in the concept of "slow alchemy": regeneration can't be rushed. We research, trial, and share openly. Did you know our intern program has trained over 150 aspiring horticulturists, many now running their own community gardens? Our on-site soil lab runs free pH and microbiology tests for low-income growers, because equitable access to knowledge is a cornerstone of ecological justice.

In 2023, we launched the "Apothecary Pathways" initiative — planting medicinal herb gardens at six public schools, teaching children how to make chamomile tea and calendula salves. This intergenerational model has grown beyond expectations, with over 2,000 students participating. At YardAlchemy, we see the garden as a classroom without walls, where every earthworm and blossom tells a story of connection.

What makes our alchemy different? We reject shortcuts. We don’t sell synthetic fertilizers or "miracle" products. Instead, we guide you through crafting your own compost extracts, building soil sponges, and co-creating with nature. Our eight-week online "Green Wizardry" course has reached gardeners in 17 countries, proving that regenerative principles transcend climate zones. From arid Arizona to rainy Wales, our students report higher yields, less pest pressure, and a profound sense of belonging to the living world.

We also maintain a seed bank that focuses on climate-adapted varieties: heat-tolerant lettuce, flood-resistant squash, and perennials that build soil carbon. Every year we donate thousands of seed packets to tribal nations and community farms. As a team, we prioritize low-tech, high-impact solutions: rain barrels, hand tools, solar dehydrators, and cob-building techniques for cold frames.

The future of YardAlchemy includes a full-fledged herbal dispensary and a field-tested curriculum for regenerative design certification. But our heart remains unchanged: we believe in the transformative power of digging your hands into rich earth, watching life emerge from a seed, and knowing you are part of something ancient and essential. That is the real alchemy.

We invite you to become a co-steward — whether by planting an herb pot on your windowsill, sharing a cutting with a neighbor, or joining one of our full-moon garden walks. Together, we turn yards into sanctuaries.
